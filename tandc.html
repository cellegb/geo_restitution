Tensions and Conflicts affecting the Americas
Costa Rica:

Nicaragua and Costa Rica: The two countries have experienced border tensions in recent years, mediated by the International Court of Justice at The Hague.

Argentina:

Tensions with Britain about sovereignty of the Falkland Islands

In 2012: Argentina makes a formal complaint to the UN stating that Britain is militarising area surrounding Falkland Islands and sending nuclear missiles to area

Tensions with Brazil about unresolved territorial disputes

Canada:

Minimal tensions within their foreign relations policies → Canada intervenes typically when there are Human rights transgressions and only when armed conflicts involve several members of the international community

Brazil:

Brazil-Uruguay border dispute : the Brazilian Island and the Corner of Artigas (interfluve between the Quaraí River and the Arroyo Invernada)

Cuba:

Tense relationship with the United States

US embargo on Cuba

Restriction on Americans traveling to Cuba

Bolivia:

strained relationship with Chile (war of the pacific (1879-1904) and the Bolivian Gas conflict)

Conflict with Peru (war during the 1930s)

Haiti:

Government eliminates subsidies in 2018

Fuel prices increase by 50%

Led to protests and civil unrest

Staff member of ‘Radio Tele Ginen’ attacked in June of 2019

Journalists becoming increasing targets of violence

Opposition groups and anti-corruption protesters demand the ouster of Haitian President Jovenel Moise.

Haitians say the violence and economic stagnation stemming from a clash between the president and the opposition are worse than anything they have ever experienced

Haiti’s important history of natural disasters. The most recent being Hurricane Matthew in 2016 (category 4 storm).

Due to recent deforestation and soil erosion, the damage following this disaster was worse than in the past. Damages estimated to round up to $ 1.9 billion.
